Wildfires Fast Facts

  • From January 1 to September 8, 2020 there were 41,051 wildfires compared with 35,386 wildfires in the same period in 2019, according to the National Interagency Fire Center. About 4.7 million acres were burned in the 2020 period, compared with 4.2 million acres in 2019.
  • Wildfires or unplanned ignitions are caused by humans about 85% of the time.
  • During the current 2020 COVID-19 pandemic, fires have scorched 1,666,286 acres in 7,335 fires.
  • Research shows that changes in climate that create warmer, drier conditions, increased drought, and a longer fire season are boosting these increases in wildfire risk.

Emergency Wildfire Kit

  • Face masks or coverings
  • Three-day supply of non-perishable food and three gallons of water per person
  • Change of clothing
  • Prescriptions or special medications
  • An extra set of car keys, credit cards, cash or traveler’s checks
  • Flashlight
  • Copies of important documents (birth certificates, passports, etc.)
  • Chargers for cell phones, laptops, etc.
  • Easily carried valuables
